DIABETES MELLITUS — COMPREHENSIVE RISK ASSESSMENT GUIDE
=========================================================

TYPE 1 DIABETES:
- Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells
- Typically presents in children and young adults (ages 4-14 most common)
- Symptoms: polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, fatigue, diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)
- Always requires insulin therapy
- Risk of hypoglycemic events with insulin therapy

TYPE 2 DIABETES:
- Insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell dysfunction
- Most common form (90-95% of all diabetes cases)
- Often associated with obesity, physical inactivity, and family history
- Can be managed with lifestyle changes, oral medications, and/or insulin
- Diagnosis: Fasting glucose >= 126 mg/dL OR HbA1c >= 6.5% OR random glucose >= 200 mg/dL with symptoms

DIABETIC EMERGENCIES:
- Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA): Blood glucose > 250 mg/dL with ketonuria and metabolic acidosis. Life-threatening. Requires IV insulin drip, fluid resuscitation, and electrolyte replacement.
- Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS): Blood glucose > 600 mg/dL with severe dehydration and altered consciousness. More common in elderly Type 2 patients. Mortality rate 5-20%.
- Hypoglycemia: Blood glucose < 70 mg/dL. Symptoms: tremor, sweating, confusion, seizures. Treat with 15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate.

CHRONIC COMPLICATIONS OF DIABETES:
- Diabetic Retinopathy: Leading cause of blindness in working-age adults. Annual eye screening recommended.
- Diabetic Nephropathy: Progressive kidney damage. Monitor urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio annually. Target BP < 130/80 mmHg.
- Diabetic Neuropathy: Peripheral nerve damage causing tingling, numbness, pain in extremities. Foot examination essential at every visit.
- Cardiovascular Disease: 2-4x increased risk of heart attack and stroke. Aggressive lipid and BP management required.

GLUCOSE MONITORING TARGETS:
- Pre-meal (fasting): 80-130 mg/dL
- Post-meal (2 hours): < 180 mg/dL
- Bedtime: 100-150 mg/dL
- HbA1c Target: < 7.0% for most adults (< 8.0% for elderly with comorbidities)

MEDICATION CLASSES FOR TYPE 2 DIABETES:
- Metformin: First-line therapy. Reduces hepatic glucose production. Avoid in renal insufficiency (eGFR < 30).
- Sulfonylureas: Stimulate insulin secretion. Risk of hypoglycemia and weight gain.
- SGLT2 Inhibitors: Promote glucose excretion via urine. Cardiovascular and renal benefits. Risk of urinary tract infections.
- GLP-1 Receptor Agonists: Enhance insulin secretion, reduce appetite. Weight loss benefit. Cardiovascular protection.
- DPP-4 Inhibitors: Moderate glucose reduction. Weight-neutral. Generally well-tolerated.
- Insulin: Required when oral medications fail to achieve targets. Types: rapid-acting, short-acting, intermediate, long-acting, premixed.
