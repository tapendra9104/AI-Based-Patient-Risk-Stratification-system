MEDICAL RISK ASSESSMENT GUIDELINES
===================================

BLOOD PRESSURE CLASSIFICATION:
- Normal: Systolic < 120 AND Diastolic < 80 mmHg
- Elevated: Systolic 120-129 AND Diastolic < 80 mmHg
- Hypertension Stage 1: Systolic 130-139 OR Diastolic 80-89 mmHg
- Hypertension Stage 2: Systolic >= 140 OR Diastolic >= 90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: Systolic > 180 AND/OR Diastolic > 120 mmHg (requires immediate medical attention)

Uncontrolled hypertension significantly increases the risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney disease, and heart failure. Patients with systolic BP above 180 or diastolic above 120 require immediate emergency evaluation.

BLOOD GLUCOSE LEVELS:
- Normal (fasting): 70-99 mg/dL
- Prediabetes (fasting): 100-125 mg/dL
- Diabetes (fasting): >= 126 mg/dL
- Normal (random): < 140 mg/dL
- Prediabetes (random): 140-199 mg/dL
- Diabetes (random): >= 200 mg/dL

Persistently elevated glucose levels lead to diabetic complications including neuropathy, nephropathy, retinopathy, and cardiovascular disease. Glucose above 300 mg/dL may indicate diabetic ketoacidosis, which is a medical emergency.

HEART RATE CLASSIFICATION:
- Bradycardia: < 60 bpm (may be normal in athletes)
- Normal resting: 60-100 bpm
- Tachycardia: > 100 bpm
- Dangerous tachycardia: > 150 bpm (requires immediate evaluation)

Abnormal heart rates combined with symptoms like chest pain, shortness of breath, or dizziness significantly increase cardiovascular risk and require prompt medical evaluation.

AGE-RELATED RISK FACTORS:
- Ages 0-30: Generally lower cardiovascular risk unless other factors present
- Ages 31-50: Moderate risk; lifestyle factors become more significant
- Ages 51-65: Elevated risk; regular screening recommended
- Ages 65+: High baseline risk; comorbidities are common and compound risk

Risk increases with age due to natural vascular changes, accumulated damage from risk factors, and declining organ function. Age above 65 is an independent risk factor for cardiovascular events.

COMBINED RISK FACTORS:
When multiple risk factors are present simultaneously, the overall risk is multiplicative rather than additive. For example:
- High BP + High Glucose + Age > 60 = Very High Risk
- High BP + Normal Glucose + Age < 50 = Moderate Risk
- Normal BP + High Glucose + Sedentary Lifestyle = Moderate Risk
- High BP + High Glucose + Smoking + Family History = Critical Risk

SYMPTOM-BASED RISK INDICATORS:
- Chest pain (especially with exertion): High cardiovascular risk
- Shortness of breath at rest: Indicates possible heart failure or pulmonary condition
- Sudden severe headache: May indicate stroke or hypertensive emergency
- Blurred vision with high BP: Target organ damage
- Frequent urination with high glucose: Poorly controlled diabetes
- Numbness or tingling in extremities: Peripheral neuropathy
- Dizziness or fainting: May indicate cardiac arrhythmia or hypotension
- Unexplained weight loss: May indicate uncontrolled diabetes or malignancy
