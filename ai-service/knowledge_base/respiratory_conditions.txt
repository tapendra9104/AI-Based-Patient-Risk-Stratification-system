RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS AND RISK ASSESSMENT
=============================================

CHRONIC OBSTRUCTIVE PULMONARY DISEASE (COPD):
- Progressive airflow limitation that is not fully reversible
- Primary cause: smoking (85-90% of cases)
- Symptoms: chronic cough, sputum production, progressive dyspnea
- Diagnosis: Spirometry showing FEV1/FVC ratio < 0.70 post-bronchodilator

COPD SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION (GOLD):
- GOLD 1 (Mild): FEV1 >= 80% predicted
- GOLD 2 (Moderate): 50% <= FEV1 < 80% predicted
- GOLD 3 (Severe): 30% <= FEV1 < 50% predicted
- GOLD 4 (Very Severe): FEV1 < 30% predicted

COPD EXACERBATION:
- Acute worsening of respiratory symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation
- Common triggers: respiratory infections, air pollution, non-compliance with medications
- Treatment: bronchodilators, systemic corticosteroids, antibiotics if purulent sputum
- Severe exacerbation requires hospitalization: respiratory rate > 25, oxygen saturation < 90%, confusion

ASTHMA ASSESSMENT:
- Reversible airway obstruction with bronchial hyperresponsiveness
- Symptoms: wheezing, cough, chest tightness, shortness of breath (worse at night or early morning)
- Triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air, respiratory infections, stress
- Severity: intermittent, mild persistent, moderate persistent, severe persistent
- Peak flow monitoring: < 50% of personal best suggests severe exacerbation

PNEUMONIA RISK FACTORS:
- Age > 65 years: 5-10x higher incidence and mortality
- Immunosuppression: HIV, transplant, chemotherapy, chronic corticosteroid use
- Chronic lung disease: COPD, bronchiectasis, cystic fibrosis
- Smoking and alcohol abuse
- Aspiration risk: dysphagia, altered consciousness, GERD

PNEUMONIA SEVERITY (CURB-65 Score):
- Confusion: new mental confusion
- Urea: > 7 mmol/L (BUN > 19 mg/dL)
- Respiratory rate: >= 30 breaths/minute
- Blood pressure: systolic < 90 OR diastolic <= 60 mmHg
- Age >= 65 years
- Score 0-1: Outpatient treatment
- Score 2: Consider short hospitalization
- Score 3-5: Hospitalization required (4-5: consider ICU)

PULMONARY EMBOLISM (PE):
- Blood clot in pulmonary arteries. Often originates from deep vein thrombosis (DVT).
- Symptoms: sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, hemoptysis
- Risk factors: recent surgery, immobilization, cancer, pregnancy, oral contraceptives, previous VTE
- Wells Score helps calculate pre-test probability
- Diagnosis: CT pulmonary angiography (gold standard), D-dimer (to rule out in low-probability patients)
- Treatment: anticoagulation (heparin bridge to warfarin or direct oral anticoagulants)
- Massive PE with hemodynamic instability: systemic thrombolysis or surgical embolectomy
