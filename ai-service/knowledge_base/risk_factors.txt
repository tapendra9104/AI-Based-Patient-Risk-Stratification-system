CARDIOVASCULAR RISK FACTORS AND ASSESSMENT
=============================================

MAJOR CARDIOVASCULAR RISK FACTORS:
1. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
   - Most common modifiable risk factor
   - Doubles cardiovascular risk for every 20/10 mmHg increase above 115/75
   - Target: < 130/80 mmHg for most adults

2. Diabetes Mellitus
   - Increases cardiovascular risk 2-4x compared to non-diabetic individuals
   - HbA1c above 7% indicates poor glycemic control
   - Diabetic patients often have silent ischemia (no chest pain symptoms)

3. Dyslipidemia (Abnormal Cholesterol)
   - Total cholesterol > 240 mg/dL is high risk
   - LDL ("bad" cholesterol) > 160 mg/dL increases risk
   - HDL ("good" cholesterol) < 40 mg/dL is a risk factor
   - Triglycerides > 200 mg/dL increases risk

4. Smoking
   - Increases cardiovascular risk by 2-4x
   - Damages blood vessel lining and promotes plaque buildup
   - Risk decreases within 1 year of quitting

5. Obesity
   - BMI > 30 increases cardiovascular risk
   - Abdominal obesity (waist circumference) is especially dangerous
   - Associated with insulin resistance and metabolic syndrome

6. Physical Inactivity
   - Sedentary lifestyle increases cardiovascular risk by 50%
   - Recommended: 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week
   - Even light walking reduces risk significantly

7. Family History
   - First-degree relative with premature CVD increases risk
   - Premature CVD: Male < 55 years, Female < 65 years

METABOLIC SYNDROME:
Defined as having 3 or more of:
- Waist circumference > 102 cm (men) or > 88 cm (women)
- Triglycerides >= 150 mg/dL
- HDL cholesterol < 40 mg/dL (men) or < 50 mg/dL (women)
- Blood pressure >= 130/85 mmHg
- Fasting glucose >= 100 mg/dL

Metabolic syndrome increases cardiovascular risk 2x and diabetes risk 5x.

CLINICAL DECISION GUIDELINES:
- Low Risk (< 10% 10-year risk): Lifestyle modification, routine follow-up in 6-12 months
- Moderate Risk (10-20% 10-year risk): Lifestyle modification plus consider pharmacotherapy, follow-up in 3-6 months
- High Risk (> 20% 10-year risk): Aggressive treatment with medication, lifestyle changes, and close follow-up within 1-4 weeks

EMERGENCY INDICATORS (Require Immediate Action):
- Systolic BP > 180 or Diastolic BP > 120 with symptoms
- Blood glucose > 400 mg/dL
- Heart rate > 150 bpm at rest with symptoms
- Chest pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or back
- Sudden onset of neurological symptoms (speech difficulty, facial drooping)
- Severe shortness of breath at rest
